What is mass shooting?

Mass shootings are defined by studies as having four or more victims and don't include gang killings or slayings that involve the death of multiple family members.
When it comes to that topic, the United States is tragically exceptional: there are more public mass shootings in the United States than in any other country in the world, according to several studies

What's behind all these mass shootings in the US?

Many of the shooters in the United States were mentally ill, according to the data. But studies have shown that the estimated number of cases of mental illness hasn't gone up significantly while the number of mass shootings in the U.S. has skyrocketed.

Number of Victims

Acoording to a study by Adam Lankford, in the United States, there are 6.87 victims on average per incident. In the other 171 countries the average was 8.8 victims per incident.

Comparing the numbers

This chart can help you to compare the number of occurrences, victims and fatalities of each state. Each circle represents a state, the larger the circle the greater the number of deaths.
The Las Vegas strip mass shooting in June 2016 was one of the worst mass shots in US history, with 585 victims, is the main reason why Nevada is the state with the most victims. California is the state with the most occurrences and deaths in recent years.
